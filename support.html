Frequently asked questions

I'm not receiving dose reminders. What should I check?

First, make sure Dose Reminders are turned on in the app under Settings → Notifications. Then check your iPhone's Settings app → Notifications → Vessalia Health and confirm notifications are allowed. If you use Focus modes (like Do Not Disturb or Sleep), add Vessalia Health to the allowed apps so reminders come through.

How do I mark a dose as taken from the notification?

Press and hold the reminder notification (or pull it down), and you'll see three actions: Mark as Taken, Snooze, and Skip. You can log the dose without ever opening the app.

How do I edit or pause a medication?

Open the Medications tab and tap a medication's card, or press and hold it for quick actions. From there you can Edit any detail (dose, schedule, color, supply), or Pause the medication to stop reminders without deleting your history. Resume anytime the same way.

How do daily health check-ins work?

Turn on Health Tracking in Settings, and a Daily Check-in card appears on Today. Tap Log to open your short list of recent and starred measures, then pick one to enter it on its own page — weight, blood pressure, glucose, mood, pain, or any measure you create yourself. Tap the star to keep a measure handy, and find everything else under "Add a Measure." Side effects have their own card, and your conditions and appointments live on Today too.

Does Vessalia Health work with Apple Health?

Yes, optionally. In Settings → Health Tracking, turn on "Sync with Apple Health" and grant permission. Weight, blood pressure, and glucose then sync both ways with Apple Health; mood, pain, and custom measures stay in the app. Sync is off until you enable it, runs entirely through Apple's on-device HealthKit, and you can turn it off or revoke access anytime.

Can I track medications for more than one person?

Yes. Turn on Caregiver Mode in Settings to create up to five separate profiles, each with its own medications, schedules, and history. Switch between them from the top of any main screen. Turn Caregiver Mode off and everything collapses back to a single profile — your data is kept, just hidden.

How does supply / refill tracking work?

Turn on "Track supply" when adding or editing a medication and enter how many doses you have. The count goes down automatically each time you log a dose. When it reaches your low-supply threshold (set in Settings → Notifications), you'll get a refill reminder. Add a refill from the medication's detail page.

Can I export my data?

Yes. Go to Settings → Data. You can export your dose history as a CSV spreadsheet or your complete data (medications, health check-ins, and history) as JSON, and share it anywhere — email, Files, AirDrop, or print for your doctor.

How do I delete my data?

Settings → Data → Clear All Data permanently deletes all medications, health data, and history from your device. Deleting the app does the same. Because nothing is ever stored outside your device, there's nothing else to delete anywhere. Anything you previously synced to Apple Health stays in Apple Health — remove it there if you'd like.

Is my health information private?

Completely. All data is stored only on your iPhone. Vessalia Health has no servers, no account system, no analytics, and no third-party advertising or tracking services. Nothing you enter ever leaves your device unless you export it yourself or turn on optional Apple Health sync, which runs entirely through Apple's on-device Health. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Does Face ID lock protect my data?

When enabled (Settings → Security), Vessalia Health requires Face ID, Touch ID, or your device passcode every time the app opens. Authentication is handled entirely by iOS — the app never sees or stores your biometric data.

Can I use it for vitamins and supplements?

Absolutely. Add anything you take on a schedule — prescriptions, vitamins, supplements, drops, injections, or inhalers — each with its own form, dose, schedule, color, and icon.
