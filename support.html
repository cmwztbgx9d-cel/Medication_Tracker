Frequently asked questions

I'm not receiving dose reminders. What should I check?

First, make sure Dose Reminders are turned on in the app under Settings → Notifications. Then check your iPhone's Settings app → Notifications → Medication Tracker and confirm notifications are allowed. If you use Focus modes (like Do Not Disturb or Sleep), add Medication Tracker to the allowed apps so reminders come through.

How do I mark a dose as taken from the notification?

Press and hold the reminder notification (or pull it down), and you'll see three actions: Mark as Taken, Snooze, and Skip. You can log the dose without ever opening the app.

How do I edit or pause a medication?

Open the Medications tab, tap the medication's card, then tap the ••• button in the top right. Choose Edit to change any detail (dose, schedule, color, supply), or Pause Medication to stop reminders without deleting your history. Resume anytime the same way.

How does supply / refill tracking work?

Turn on "Track supply" when adding or editing a medication and enter how many doses you have. The count goes down automatically each time you log a dose. When it reaches your low-supply threshold (set in Settings → Notifications), you'll get a refill reminder. Add a refill from the medication's detail page.

Can I export my data?

Yes. Go to Settings → Data. You can export your dose history as a CSV spreadsheet or your complete data (medications and history) as JSON, and share it anywhere — email, Files, AirDrop, or print for your doctor.

How do I delete my data?

Settings → Data → Clear All Data permanently deletes all medications and history from your device. Deleting the app does the same. Because nothing is ever stored outside your device, there's nothing else to delete anywhere.

Is my health information private?

Completely. All data is stored only on your iPhone. Medication Tracker has no servers, no account system, no analytics, and no third-party services. Nothing you enter ever leaves your device unless you export it yourself. See our Privacy Policy for details.

Does Face ID lock protect my data?

When enabled (Settings → Security), Medication Tracker requires Face ID, Touch ID, or your device passcode every time the app opens. Authentication is handled entirely by iOS — the app never sees or stores your biometric data.

Can I use it for vitamins and supplements?

Absolutely. Add anything you take on a schedule — prescriptions, vitamins, supplements, drops, injections, or inhalers — each with its own form, dose, schedule, color, and icon.
